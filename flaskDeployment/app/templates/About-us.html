Conclusion

Out of our three models, K-Nearest Neighbors, Random Forest Classifier, and Decision Tree Classifier, we found that the most accurate model was the Random Forest Classifier with a 70% accuracy. We also found that not changing the NaN values made it more accurate than imputing the NaN values. If we had more time we could try different imputing techniques to improve our accuracy or gather more data to have a better trained models. If we were to change this problem to a regression problem, instead of classifying whether the water is potable enough to drink, we could scale water potability to usage. So you could use water for different purposes depending on the waters quality.

So What?

We created this project because we want people to be able to know if they have a clean water source or not. Our hope is that this product will help millions of people around the world who live in the the poorer areas determine if their water source is truly sanitary water or not, so people won't start getting sick from drinking dirty water. It does all of this by predicting the waters potability from thousands of datapoints!
